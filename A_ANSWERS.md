# 2. Describe a situation you had to resolve with a teammate from a study or work group. 

During my "Technological Entrepreneurships" course last year, we worked in a team using **Agile methodologies**. We organized our workflow in 2-week Sprints, managing our backlog in trello, communicating via Slack, and delivering our visual work on Mural.

Our workflow required us to finish our task before Friday so the professor could review our Mural board in the morning, right before our Sprint Review. However, one teammate consistently neglected the schedule because he priorized other subjects. He would only do his part at the last minute during de review day. As a result, his work was never evaluated by the professor, which negatively impacted the team´s overall feedback.

First, we tried talking to him multiple times to explain how his delays affected the professor´s review process and the team´s performance, but his attitud remained dismissive. Since dialogue wasn´t working, we took a structural approach. We formally designated one of the other team members as Scrum Master and asked the professor to grant them administrative permissions to lock the Mural board on the morning of the Sprint Review. We clearly comminicated this new hard boundary to the teammate: no last-minute edits would be allowed, and we would not do his unfinished work for him.

Although he was initially upset because he didn´t consider the elective course a priority, the strict deadline forced him to adapt. He  adjusted his behavior, started completing his task on time before tha board was locked, and our team was finally able to receive complete and accurate feedback during the Sprint Review. The consensus of the majority prevalied, and we solved the issue professionally.
